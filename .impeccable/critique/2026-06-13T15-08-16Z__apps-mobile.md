## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: The app does not read as pure AI slop; it has real domain structure, backend-aware statuses, ticket/course/event concepts, and reusable components. The tell is that it feels like two visual systems stitched together: the newer clinical light UI in auth/forms, and older dark glass cards in home/community/courses/tickets/profile. That split weakens trust for a medical education product.

**Deterministic scan**: `detect.mjs --json apps/mobile` returned `[]`. The detector did not flag the mobile-specific issues found manually, especially hard-coded dark React Native styles and component-system drift.

**Visual overlays**: No reliable user-visible overlay was available. Browser automation could open the Expo web build, but the available Playwright tools did not expose the mutable script injection and console APIs required by the Impeccable overlay flow.

## Overall Impression

AMG Academy is close to having a credible product spine: dentists can understand events, courses, tickets, certificates, and profile setup. The biggest opportunity is to make it feel like one clinical event passport instead of a patched redesign. The app should feel lighter, calmer, more medically precise, and more explicit at every high-trust moment.

## What's Working

- The product model is strong: events, reservations, QR tickets, certificates, courses, scanner, and profile completion are the right domain surfaces.
- Form improvements are meaningful: required marks, optional professional metadata, phone field structure, and friendlier error mapping are moving in the right direction.
- Shared states and cards exist, so the app has enough component foundation to polish system-wide instead of fixing every screen by hand.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] The app still has two visual identities

**Why it matters**: The documented direction rejects heavy near-black UI, but core tabs still use dark translucent hero/cards. This makes payment, ticket, and learning states feel less trustworthy and less connected to AMG’s clinical premium brand.

**Fix**: Replace old dark overrides with documented light/dark tokens. Rename or neutralize `GlassCard` so the component vocabulary says “clinical card,” not decorative glass.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable colorize apps/mobile`

### [P1] High-stakes errors are still too vague

**Why it matters**: The user already saw “Something went wrong / Please try.” For registration, Google profile completion, login, payment, and tickets, vague recovery text creates support load and undermines backend trust.

**Fix**: Make errors specific by state: invalid profile field, expired session, network unavailable, duplicate email, payment pending, QR not issued, etc. Keep the user’s form data intact.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable clarify apps/mobile`

### [P1] Profile completion is too much form at once

**Why it matters**: A dentist coming from Google sign-in expects to continue quickly. Showing name, phone, specialty, clinic, city, title, practice type, years, errors, and buttons in one vertical stack creates abandonment risk.

**Fix**: Split into required professional identity first, optional practice details second, or visually group required vs optional fields. Put the “why this is needed” message near the required group.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout apps/mobile/src/features/auth/GoogleProfileCompletion.tsx`

### [P1] Theming is configured but not truly designed through the app

**Why it matters**: `userInterfaceStyle` is automatic, but color choice is static at module load and many screens override tokens manually. Users changing theme can get inconsistent screens.

**Fix**: Move active color scheme into a theme provider/hook and make styles consume the current scheme consistently. Remove hard-coded legacy rgba values.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable audit apps/mobile`

### [P2] Event/course/ticket cards overuse the same visual grammar

**Why it matters**: Repeated cards with uppercase kickers, badges, pills, shadows, and multiple status elements make the app feel busier than the task requires. Users need to scan “Can I attend? Do I owe money? Where is my ticket?” faster.

**Fix**: Give each domain a clearer primary fact: event date/action, course progress/access, ticket validity/QR. Reduce decorative metadata and standardize card density.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable distill apps/mobile/src/components/cards`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer Dentist)**: The first login/register flow is understandable, but profile completion asks for several professional fields without explaining which ones unlock event/course access. “Required” errors are technically clear but not reassuring.

**Sam (Accessibility-Dependent User)**: Many buttons have labels, which is good. Red flags remain around unlabeled/decorative images, ID-like accessibility hints in `TextField`, and switches that need explicit labels/states.

**Casey (Distracted Mobile User)**: The app is mobile-first, but long forms plus a startup video plus multiple stacked cards make interruption recovery fragile. Casey needs fewer required decisions per screen and clearer “you can finish later” signals where allowed.

**Event Staff Operator**: Scanner and ticket validation need brutal clarity. Any dark decorative card, vague invalid state, or slow startup animation is more costly during a live check-in line than during casual browsing.

## Minor Observations

- `GlassCard` is now semantically misleading even when visually softened.
- The home title at 32px exceeds the documented product scale and can make dashboard content feel more like a landing page.
- Google red is acceptable as brand color, but danger/status tints should come from semantic tokens.
- Uppercase kickers appear often; one or two can work, but repeated across cards they become product noise.
- The phone field is much improved, but should be stress-tested at large text sizes and narrow screens.

## Questions to Consider

- What should a dentist understand in the first 5 seconds after opening the app: next event, active ticket, current course, or profile completeness?
- Should profile completion feel like credential verification or like ordinary account setup?
- What visual element should carry “AMG event energy” if dark glass cards are removed?
- Which state needs the most trust: payment, QR ticket, certificate, or course access?
