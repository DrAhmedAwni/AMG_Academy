## Users

Dentists, dental professionals, instructors, event staff, and AMG Academy administrators use this platform around real professional development workflows. Dentists need to discover and register for AMG events, workshops, congress activities, and courses; manage payments and tickets; access learning material; receive updates; and prove eligibility at check-in or through certificates. Staff and authorized users need reliable scanning, validation, and operational views that support live events without slowing the line.

## Product Purpose

AMG Academy is the education and events product from AMG Medical Group, a dental medical devices company. It connects AMG's professional dental audience to courses, workshops, events, certificates, notifications, tickets, and scanner flows through a mobile-first experience backed by the AMG Academy web and API platform.

Success means dentists can confidently register, enroll, pay, attend, learn, and return to their records without confusion; AMG teams can operate events and learning programs with accurate backend-controlled state; and the product reinforces AMG as a credible medical education and dental technology brand.

## Brand Personality

Clinical premium, trustworthy, and energetic.

The product should feel like a professional dental education platform from a serious medical devices company: polished, precise, clear, and credible. It can carry the energy of events and workshops, but that energy should never compromise readability, trust, or task completion.

## Anti-references

Avoid heavy near-black interfaces that make forms and event flows feel gloomy or hard to read. Avoid generic SaaS dashboards, decorative glassmorphism, over-rounded controls, unclear form requirements, vague error messages, and flashy event visuals that make the product feel less medical or less trustworthy. Avoid childish learning-app styling, cluttered promotional layouts, and any UI that makes dentists wonder whether payment, ticket, course, or certificate state is reliable.

## Design Principles

1. Put workflow clarity before decoration. Registration, enrollment, payment, tickets, certificates, and scanner results must be obvious and fast to understand.
2. Earn medical trust. Use restrained typography, clear hierarchy, reliable status language, and polished but practical components.
3. Show event energy in controlled moments. Use AMG brand marks, teal/cyan accents, and confident spacing to make the platform feel active without overwhelming forms or operational screens.
4. Make backend authority visible. Payment, eligibility, QR, certificate, course access, and role states should read as server-confirmed, not guessed by the client.
5. Design for repeated use. Dentists and staff should be able to scan, compare, submit, and recover from errors without relearning the interface each time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA as the baseline for user-facing screens. Maintain readable contrast in light and dark appearances, 44px or larger tap targets, accessible labels for icon-only controls, clear required-field marks, plain-language validation messages, and reduced-motion-safe transitions. Forms should remain usable with mobile keyboards, long names, international phone numbers, and event-day pressure.
